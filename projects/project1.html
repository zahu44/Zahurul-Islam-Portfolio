Project Abstract
The maps collectively illustrate a highly dynamic channel behavior of the Padma River over the past five decades, marked by significant lateral migration, erosion, and accretion processes. Between 1975 and 2025, extensive erosion (orange) dominates along the outer banks, particularly downstream of the Padma Bridge, while accretion (light blue) occurs intermittently on inner bends and mid-channel bars, reflecting typical meander evolution. The 2015–2025 comparison shows accelerated erosion in certain reaches, suggesting increased channel instability in recent years. The multi-temporal bankline map further highlights continuous shifting, with the river migrating both laterally and longitudinally, reshaping its planform and affecting surrounding land use. Overall, the study indicates an active and unstable fluvial system where erosion predominates over accretion in many sections, posing ongoing risks to infrastructure and nearby settlements.
